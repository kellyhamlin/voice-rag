# SIP 503 on a Trunk

A 503 means the next hop is refusing the request, usually due to load or an
administrative/overload condition.

## Common causes
- Overload control (the far end is shedding traffic).
- Trunk/route admin-down or out of licensed channels.
- Options ping failing, so the route is marked unavailable.

## Steps
1. Check whether 503 is constant or load-correlated.
2. Verify OPTIONS keepalives are succeeding to the next hop.
3. Confirm available channels/licenses on the trunk group.
4. Coordinate with the far-end carrier if overload persists.
